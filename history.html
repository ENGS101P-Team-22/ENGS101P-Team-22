History & Important Figures

The concept of Virtual Reality can be considered to be more aged than the first materialisation of a computer. The early cinematographers and stage managers where thinking on how to draw the audience into the play more effectively. They thought that if stimulating the senses of the audience - vision, sound, smell even touch(with the use of wind) they will shorten the distance between the real and the imaginary(at that time) world. The development of computers implemented the Virtual Environments and Graphics in a lot of spheres, but the concept of using the senses(sight, hearing etc.) to make the environment as close and accurate as the real-life scenario is what defines Virtual Environment and Graphics branch of Computer Science.

One of the earliest virtual worlds built was the Ivan Sutherland's virtual reality simulator. These kind of devices are characterised by huge headsets and other type of sensory input simulation. These machines were focused on stimulating 2 or even 3 senses at a time which, taking in consideration the power of computers and the level of functionality of the devices, was very challenging.

Another significant implementation of VR and Graphics was the first 3D multi-user, networked first person shooter game Maze War developed by Steve Colley and Greg Thomson. The developers focused only on stimulating vision, by introducing motion in a 3D virtual environment. The player gets engaged in the game by analysing the visual content the screen offers.

The evolving hardware and software industries made possible implementation of Virtual Environments such that the user/audience gets engaged in the virtual scenario not only at the physiological-sense level but at the emotional as well, and that's why we as a team consider this research area to be interesting.
